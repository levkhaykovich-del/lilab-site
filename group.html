Principal Investigator

Prof. Lev Khaykovich

Full Professor · Department of Physics · Bar-Ilan University

Lev Khaykovich founded the Ultracold Atoms Laboratory at Bar-Ilan University in 2004. His research focuses on the experimental study of ultracold quantum gases, with a particular emphasis on universal few-body physics, matter-wave solitons, and — in the lab's new chapter — higher-order many-body effects and ultracold chemistry.

His group made foundational contributions to Efimov physics, including early observations of Efimov states in lithium and the development of interferometric techniques for probing few-body quantum states with unprecedented precision. The lab has published over 50 papers in leading journals including Physical Review Letters and Nature Communications.

Prof. Khaykovich actively collaborates with leading groups at ENS Paris, JILA (University of Colorado), and the University of Colorado, and has mentored numerous students and postdocs who have gone on to positions in academia and industry worldwide.
